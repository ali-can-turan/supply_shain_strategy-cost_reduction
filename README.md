Project: Supply Chain Cost Optimization Strategy (Post-Excise Tax Alleviation)

This project aims to alleviate the affliction of newly-put excise tax on medical firms' revenues by cost reduction, to find fields where such cost reduction could be realized for the Company X.

-Overview:

This project focuses on a strategic cost-reduction initiative for a medical device company ("Company X") facing a 2.3% Excise Tax on revenues. Given that this tax translates to a 10-20% reduction in net profit, the study analyzes operational inefficiencies and identifies areas within SG&A (Selling, General, and Administrative) expenses where significant savings can be realized.

This case is normally presented as a capstone project in “Supply Chain Management Specialization” administered by Rutgers University on www.coursera.org. There was not and cannot be one true solution to the case below. This is the version of how I would approach to and solve the case.

--Key Analyses & Findings:

Demand Forecasting: Evaluated erratic demand patterns and implemented a "Yearly Naive Forecast" model, successfully reaching that demand variation between the last two years to be only 3.1%.

Inventory Optimization: Proposed the elimination of "Trunk Storage" in favor of an RFID-integrated centralized system, aiming to reduce safety stock levels from 130,000 to 30,000 units.

Logistics & Distribution: Developed a "disintermediation" strategy to reduce distributor dependency, shifting towards 3PL (Third-Party Logistics) and direct retail sales to hospitals.

Organizational Transformation: Recommended centralizing administrative functions and decoupling sales representatives from logistical tasks to optimize fleet costs.

--Results & Strategic Recommendations:

* Minimization of forecasting errors by implementing the Yearly Naive method for erratic demand.

* Elimination of the "Trunk Storage" model through RFID integration to ensure real-time inventory visibility.

* Optimization of holding costs by reducing safety stock from 130,000 to approximately 30,000 units.

* Reduction in SG&A expenses by narrowing the scope of sales reps’ roles and optimizing vehicle fleet costs.

* Increased profit margins via a "Direct-to-Hospital" (Retail) model, bypassing high-cost distributor channels.

* Streamlined operations through 3PL outsourcing for warehousing and specialized logistics.

* Consolidation of administrative units to leverage economies of scale at the central production hub.
